About us

The CASSIOPEIA project, funded by NGI 3rd Trust Call, with the duration of 6 months, will provide a proof-of-concept for policymakers, technologists and the public showing how privacy-by-design can mean enhanced informational control - focusing on sharing rather than hiding data.

Concepts

Our work is based on transparent handling of personal data.

Selected Sharing

Selective sharing is a critical dimension of privacy: enhancing user choice, autonomy, participation, and trust. It is the technical embodiment of respect for social contexts in information sharing. Moreover, “privacy-by-default and -design” is the law of the land, but there are few examples of what that actually means aside from basic ideas of confidentiality and limited conceptions of transparency.

Consent Receipts

Individuals can use the devices and access the data, but they cannot repudiate the permission previously granted, and the consent receipts act as a formal proof to external parties.
