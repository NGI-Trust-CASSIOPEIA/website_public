Vision

Open-standard/open-source technologies can be used to create usable and transparent architectures enabling device owners to selectively collect, share and retain data from users, while delegating control of device features to the users from whom data is being obtained. “privacy-by-default and -design” is the law of the land, but there are few examples of what that actually means aside from basic ideas of confidentiality and limited conceptions of transparency.

Concepts

Airbnb Scenario

Where a host wants to rent an apartment containing IoT devices to a guest, which involves the delegation of device functions to the guest and management of the guest’s collected personal data

Selective Sharing

A critical dimension of privacy: enhancing user choice, autonomy, participation, and trust. It is the technical embodiment of respect for social contexts in information sharing

Consent Receipts

The means to represent user authorization to process personal data and to frame data handling practices, with support for data retention and non repudiation
